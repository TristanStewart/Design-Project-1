All Three Parts of the Shoulders

The shoulder muscles, known as the deltoids, are comprised of three
distinct parts. The front deltoid, situated at the front of the shoulder,
plays a role in shoulder flexion and assists in shoulder abduction
The middle deltoid, located in the middle part of the shoulder,
is primarily responsible for shoulder abduction, aiding in lifting the arm sideways.
Lastly, the rear deltoid, positioned at the back of the shoulder, is involved in shoulder
extension, horizontal abduction, and external rotation.
